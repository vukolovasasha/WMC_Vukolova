Why do people in medieval illustrations look ridiculous

Welcome to medieval humanity, lovingly misdrawn.

A place where anatomy is more of a suggestion, faces ignore basic physics, and everyone looks vaguely guilty of something. These paintings were meant to capture the soul — because clearly the body was optional. Legs bend in mysterious directions, hands resemble bread, and expressions range from “eternal confusion” to “I have seen the end of time.”
Why do medieval people look like this?
Honestly — it doesn’t really matter.
We could blame missing anatomy textbooks, spiritual symbolism, or the fact that perspective was still “coming soon.”
But let’s be real: the answer changes nothing.
What does matter is this — we live in a different century. One with mirrors, cameras, anatomy classes, and the basic understanding that humans have joints and necks.
